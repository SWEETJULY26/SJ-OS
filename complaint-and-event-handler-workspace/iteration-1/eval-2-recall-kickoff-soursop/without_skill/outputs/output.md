## 2. What I'd flag before calling this a confirmed recall

A recall is a serious, resource-heavy action — pulling stock, notifying retailers, possibly notifying customers and the FDA. Before locking that in, a few things are worth checking, and I don't have the data in front of me to confirm them:

- Are all 4 complaints actually the same lot (24-11), confirmed by the complaint records, or is that lot number self-reported by customers and unverified?
- Is there a known formula, packaging, or process change specific to lot 24-11 (e.g., new supplier ingredient, filling line issue) that would explain a lot-specific defect vs. a broader Soursop Vit C issue?
- Have the two medical-visit cases been logged as adverse events yet? If not, they need SAE triage in parallel — that's a distinct clock and reporting path from the recall decision.
- What's the current distribution/inventory picture for lot 24-11 — how much is still in the warehouse, on retail shelves (Sephora/Ulta/Whole Foods), and already in customers' hands via DTC?

None of that blocks starting recall prep — it just determines scope (single lot vs. broader hold) and urgency (immediate stop-ship vs. staged).
