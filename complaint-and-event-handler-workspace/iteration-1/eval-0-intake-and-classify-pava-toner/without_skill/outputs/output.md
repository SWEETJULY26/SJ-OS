# Complaint Intake Plan — Pava Toner, Lot 25-03

## 1. Summary of what came in

Customer reports facial redness and itching after using the Pava Toner, lot 25-03. She is requesting a refund. No mention (yet) of medical treatment sought, duration of symptoms, or whether she stopped use.

## 2. Classification (recommended — needs your sign-off)

- **Type:** Product complaint — adverse skin reaction (contact irritation/allergic-type reaction reported by customer)
- **Severity (draft):** Potential Adverse Event — Mild/Moderate, pending more detail. Redness + itching without reported medical visit, swelling, blistering, or systemic symptoms would typically sit below the threshold for a "serious" adverse event, but that determination needs the missing facts below before it's final.
- **Product/Batch:** Pava Toner, Lot 25-03
- **Reporter:** End customer (direct-to-consumer channel — confirm exact intake source: website form, email, social, retailer passthrough)
- **Trend check needed:** Before closing classification, check whether any other complaints exist against Lot 25-03 or the Pava Toner SKU in the last 90 days. A single isolated report reads very differently than a second or third report on the same lot.

## 3. Information gaps to close before final classification

Ask the customer (or check the original complaint form) for:
1. Did she stop using the product immediately? Is she still symptomatic?
2. Did she seek medical attention (doctor visit, ER, dermatologist)?
3. Any swelling, blistering, hives, or spreading beyond the application area?
4. When did symptoms start relative to first use (same day, after repeated use)?
5. Photos of the reaction, if she's willing to share.
6. Confirm the lot number directly from the bottle (customer-reported lot numbers are sometimes misread).
7. Any known allergies or new products used around the same time (to rule out confounders).

## 4. First-response recommendation (draft — do not send without approval)

Tone: empathetic, non-defensive, no admission of fault, no diagnosis.

> Hi [Name],
>
> Thank you for letting us know, and I'm sorry to hear about the reaction you had. Your comfort and safety matter to us, and we want to make this right.
>
> A few quick questions so we can look into this properly:
> - Have you stopped using the product?
> - Are you still experiencing redness or itching, and has it spread or worsened?
> - Did you see a doctor or dermatologist for it?
>
> In the meantime, we'll process your refund for the Pava Toner — no need to return the product, but if you're able to share a photo of the reaction and hold onto the bottle (including the lot number on the bottom), it would help our quality team look into the batch.
>
> We take this seriously and will follow up as soon as we've looked into it further.

Notes:
- Refund: recommend approving regardless of investigation outcome — standard practice for a reported adverse reaction complaint, and it de-risks the interaction while classification is pending.
- Do not admit product defect or liability in the response.
- Do not tell her the product is "safe" or dismiss the reaction.
