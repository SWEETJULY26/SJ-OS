# LANEIGE

**Profile status**: SKELETON — aspirational tier, lighter refresh cadence (full population in Q1 teardown, abbreviated reads in off-quarters).

**Last refreshed**: April 2026 (skeleton)

---

## 1. Identity

Amorepacific Korean beauty brand, founded 1994, global expansion through the 2010s. US prestige presence anchored in Sephora and Ulta. Known globally for the Water Sleeping Mask and Lip Sleeping Mask.

**Positioning**: hydration-forward K-beauty prestige. Broader category than SJS (skincare-first but with sleep-mask halo).

**Why LANEIGE is aspirational, not direct**: LANEIGE is not a peer. Different parent structure (Amorepacific conglomerate), different tier economics (masstige leaning premium), different cultural context (Korean beauty prestige positioning). But they own the prestige lip balm category at Ulta and any new lip launch at Ulta is benchmarked against the Lip Sleeping Mask. Ignoring LANEIGE means ignoring the shelf reality SJS lip will compete on.

## 2. Retail footprint

- Sephora: full line, US and globally
- Ulta: full line, strong merchandising

## 3. Portfolio at a glance

- SKU count: 40+ globally, ~25 US active
- Category mix: skincare primary, lip subset (Lip Sleeping Mask line + Lip Glowy Balm), body light
- Hero SKUs: Lip Sleeping Mask ($24), Water Sleeping Mask ($29), Water Bank Blue Hyaluronic line
- LE strategy: heavy on Lip Sleeping Mask flavor/limited-edition drops. This is the pattern Rhode and SJS should study.

## 4. Price ladder

- Lip entry: ~$24 (Lip Sleeping Mask)
- Lip Glowy Balm: ~$18
- Skincare core: $29 to $65
- Premium tier: Water Bank line, $40 to $65

Validate exact current pricing during teardown. LANEIGE rarely moves MSRP but does run deeper Ulta promotional participation than most prestige brands — worth tracking.

## 5. Hero SKUs deep read

Lip Sleeping Mask — the single most important SKU in prestige lip. Populate full read during Q1 teardown.

## 6. Pipeline and cadence

Faster than any direct comp. Amorepacific supports a heavy pipeline. 15+ new SKUs per year globally, 6-10 US launches. LE lip flavors are a major driver — the LSM has had 15+ flavor drops since US launch.

## 7. Social and content posture

Moderate social presence in US, very heavy in Korea. US strategy leans on creator partnerships and review velocity rather than founder halo. No founder cultural engine (LANEIGE is a brand, not a personality).

## 8. Earned media standing

Stable, high baseline. TikTok virality moments around the Lip Sleeping Mask drive periodic surges. Populate during teardown.

## 9. Strategic read

Populate during teardown. Early hypothesis: LANEIGE is the "scale what works" version of the lip-as-acquisition strategy. They proved the model years before Rhode, but at a lower price point and with far heavier category depth behind it. The LSM is not the brand — it's the gateway. SJS lip should study LANEIGE's gateway-to-portfolio conversion rate more than LANEIGE's LSM marketing.

## 10. What SJS should watch

1. LSM pricing discipline. Any MSRP change is rare and significant — same-day flag.
2. Ulta promotional tier. LANEIGE participates in Ulta promotions more than prestige peers; watching the promotional depth tells us the Ulta promo architecture SJS will be inside of.
3. New lip SKU launches. Any line extension in lip directly changes the shelf composition SJS will be positioned within.
4. Content partnerships with prestige beauty creators — signals which creators Ulta prestige audience trusts.
