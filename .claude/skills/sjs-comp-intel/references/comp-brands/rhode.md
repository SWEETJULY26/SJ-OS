## 1. Identity

Founder led celebrity skincare. Hailey Bieber's brand, launched June 2022 as a DTC-first operation with an initial three SKU line (barrier cream, peptide glazing fluid, peptide lip treatment). Acquired by e.l.f. Beauty in May 2025 for approximately $1B. Remains operationally independent under Hailey's creative direction but now benefits from e.l.f.'s retail leverage and supply chain.

**Positioning**: "Essential skincare" — minimal line, high quality, high gloss finish aesthetic. The glazed donut skin visual trademark.

**Why Rhode is our primary comp**: closest structural analog to SJS on four dimensions. Founder led by a celebrity with a cultivated personal brand (parallels Ayesha). DTC first then retail pivot (parallels our Ulta entry). Lip led acquisition strategy with the lip treatment as the entry SKU anchoring a premium skincare portfolio (directly parallels the lip-as-acquisition architecture the margin framework is codifying for SJS). Prestige price positioning with accessible entry SKU.

## 2. Retail footprint

- **DTC**: rhode.com. Still the flagship channel. Launches drop here first.
- **Sephora**: entered US Sephora in Q2 2025 post e.l.f. acquisition. Currently full line in US, expanding UK and EU.
- **Ulta**: not currently at Ulta as of April 2026. This is notable and worth watching — e.l.f.'s Ulta relationship is strong; Rhode's absence is a deliberate choice, not a rejection.
- **Amazon**: not on Amazon Premium Beauty as of April 2026. Rumored consideration late 2026.
- **International**: UK DTC and Sephora UK (2024). Sephora EU markets 2025. No Asia presence yet.

**Confidence**: high on US presence, medium-high on international.

## 3. Portfolio at a glance

- **SKU count**: 14 active as of April 2026 (up from 3 at launch, roughly 4 new SKUs per year).
- **Category mix**: skincare (8), lip (4), color (2). Color launched Q4 2024.

- **LE strategy**: frequent. Limited edition lip treatment flavors drop roughly every 8 to 12 weeks. These are marketing engines as much as SKUs.

## 5. Hero SKUs deep read

**Peptide Lip Treatment** — $18 DTC, launched June 2022. The acquisition hero. Ships in a glossy tube with a slanted applicator. Flavors rotate quarterly (Salted Caramel, Espresso, Peppermint Refresh). Estimated to carry 30-40% of brand revenue, though e.l.f. has not publicly broken this out. The Peptide Lip Treatment is the SKU competitors benchmark against in the $18-$24 premium lip space.

**Peptide Glazing Fluid** — $32, launched 2022 with initial line. The serum that made "glazed skin" a category. Has been reformulated once (2024) with minor tweaks, no structural change. Primary drivers: social proof and repeat purchase.

**Barrier Restore Cream** — $52, top of line moisturizer. Anchors the prestige tier. Launched as part of the original three SKU drop. Less cultural moment than the lip treatment or the glazing fluid, but high review velocity and strong repeat.

## 6. Pipeline and cadence

- **SKUs per year**: averaging 4 new SKUs per year, plus 4-6 limited edition lip flavors per year.
- **Category expansion pacing**: skincare-only for year one. Lip expansion year two. Color (blush, eyeliner) year three (Q4 2024). Suggested year four (2026): eye skincare and/or body. No confirmed roadmap.
- **Tease to shelf**: average 4-6 weeks from first social tease to available for purchase. Aggressive but not reckless. Hailey's personal account drives the tease moments.
- **Hero vs LE mix**: roughly 70% hero SKUs (permanent line), 30% LE (rotating flavors and seasonal drops). The LE program is structured around the lip treatment almost exclusively.

## 7. Social and content posture

- **Platform mix**: Instagram primary (9.1M followers), TikTok strong (2.3M), YouTube light.
- **Creator strategy**: minimal paid creator network. Leans heavily on earned celebrity wears (Hailey's personal feed, her friend network, organic celebrity spotting).
- **Content frequency**: ~1 post per day on brand IG, with major drops clustered around launches. Moderate TikTok presence, not TikTok-first.
- **Content style**: high production value, minimal, glossy. Product as art object. Almost no tutorial or education content.
- **What's working**: the Hailey-halo flywheel. What's a risk: the brand is structurally dependent on Hailey's cultural relevance. No observable effort to decouple.

## 9. Strategic read

Rhode is running a textbook celebrity skincare playbook with unusually high discipline. Three things to understand:

**First**, the brand is not trying to be a full portfolio skincare house. It's a premium essentials brand. Only 14 SKUs after four years. This restraint is a strategic weapon. Most celebrity brands dilute quickly. Rhode's slow cadence forces every launch to be an event.

**Second**, the lip treatment is not a lip product. It's a trojan horse. It gets a new customer into the brand at $18, builds repeat purchase habit (the flavor rotation drives this), and graduates customers to the $32 to $52 core. This is exactly the architecture the SJS margin framework is designing for, and Rhode has proven it works.

**Third**, the e.l.f. acquisition changes what to watch. Pre-acquisition, Rhode was a pure DTC story with Sephora as the first retail move. Post-acquisition, Ulta becomes a meaningful question. e.l.f. is an Ulta powerhouse. If Rhode enters Ulta in 2026 or 2027, it directly pressures the SJS Ulta launch positioning.

**Vulnerabilities**: Hailey dependency, narrow portfolio creates concentration risk on the lip treatment, no clinical depth behind formulas (claim substantiation is thin — an opportunity for SJS to differentiate).

## 10. What SJS should watch

1. **Rhode's Ulta timing.** If they enter Ulta before SJS fall 2026 launch, it compresses our launch narrative. If after, we have room to establish Ulta positioning first. Monthly watch.
2. **Lip treatment pricing.** Any MSRP move or sustained promo on the lip treatment directly pressures SJS lip pricing architecture. Within 48 hour flag if anything moves.
3. **Category expansion direction.** Where Rhode goes next (eye? body? hair?) tells us where celebrity skincare is headed next, and where SJS should be or shouldn't be.
4. **Hailey's cultural bandwidth.** If Hailey reduces personal brand activity (new baby, business focus elsewhere), Rhode's growth story changes. Relevant to understanding founder-led brand risk more broadly.
