# Comp Brand Profiles

Five brand profiles. Three direct comps (Rhode, Summer Fridays, Kosas), two aspirational (LANEIGE, YTTP).

Each profile is a living document. Updated quarterly during teardown production, plus ad hoc when a major signal lands mid-quarter.

## Profile structure

Every profile follows the same sections:

1. **Identity** — positioning one-liner, founder, parent company, launch year
2. **Retail footprint** — where they sell, by channel
3. **Portfolio at a glance** — SKU count, category mix, hero SKUs
4. **Price ladder** — current MSRP across the line, channel by channel
5. **Hero SKUs deep read** — the two or three SKUs that carry the brand
6. **Pipeline and cadence** — SKUs per year, tease-to-shelf timelines, LE strategy
7. **Social and content posture** — platform mix, creator strategy, content frequency
8. **Earned media standing** — press tier, recent moments, MIV estimate if available
9. **Strategic read** — what this brand is actually betting on, and where they're vulnerable
10. **What SJS should watch** — the two or three things to monitor closely going forward

## Profile status tier

Each profile tagged with one of:
- **FULL** — populated, currently fresh (refreshed within last 90 days)
- **FULL (stale)** — populated but not refreshed in last 90 days
- **SKELETON** — basics only, pending full population in quarterly teardown
- **AT RISK** — profile has uncovered gaps that need next-quarter attention

## When to update profile vs when to just flag in teardown

**Update profile** when the change is structural — new retailer, new hero SKU, new positioning, new parent company, founder departure.

**Flag in teardown only** when the change is tactical — a specific promo, a specific launch, a specific creator partnership. Those belong in Section 2 of the teardown, not the evergreen profile.
