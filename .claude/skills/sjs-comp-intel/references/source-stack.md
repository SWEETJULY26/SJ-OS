# Source Stack

Full catalog of data sources and tools for the CI stream, organized by cost tier. Year one recommendation: free layer plus lightweight paid layer only. Revisit heavyweight in Q1 2027.

## Free layer — always on

**Trade press (newsletters, free tier access)**
- Glossy (glossy.co) — daily. Beauty industry moves, retail, earned media.
- Business of Fashion Beauty (businessoffashion.com) — daily. Strategic and M&A angle.
- Beauty Independent (beautyindependent.com) — daily. Indie and emerging brand lens.
- BeautyMatter (beautymatter.com) — free tier. Category and brand coverage.
- Retail Dive (retaildive.com) — daily. Retailer and channel shifts.
- WWD Beauty (wwd.com) — daily. Earned media and brand moments.
- CosmeticsDesign (cosmeticsdesign.com) — weekly. Ingredient and formulation trends.
- NewHope Network — wellness and clean beauty cross-pollination.

**Retailer monitoring**
- Sephora "New" and "Just Dropped" pages — weekly scrape.
- Ulta "New Arrivals" and "New at Ulta" — weekly scrape.
- Amazon Premium Beauty "New Releases" — weekly scrape.
- Credo Beauty "New" — biweekly.

**Archival and historical**
- Wayback Machine (archive.org) — essential for reverse engineering launch timelines and old pricing.
- Brand Instagram histories — manual scroll or lightweight scraping.
- Founder podcast archives — Glossy Beauty Podcast, Beauty Independent's Dupe, Fat Mascara, How'd She Do That.

**Consumer signal**
- Reddit r/SkincareAddiction — unfiltered consumer sentiment.
- Reddit r/beautyguruchatter — category discourse.
- TikTok hashtag search — #SkincareTok, #BeautyCommunity, brand specific tags.
- Sephora and Ulta product page reviews — review velocity, sentiment, keyword patterns.

**Brand direct**
- Brand email subscriptions (every comp). Morning scan on launch days.
- Brand press rooms and PR distribution.
- Investor materials for public parent companies (e.l.f. Beauty for Rhode as of 2025 acquisition, Shiseido for Drunk Elephant, L'Oréal for YTTP and Kiehl's).

**Aggregators**
- Google Alerts on each brand name and each founder name.
- Muck Rack free tier for press coverage tracking.

**Cost: $0**

## Lightweight paid layer — recommended year one

**Trade press pro tier**
- Glossy Pro — ~$500 a year. Deeper analyst content, industry reports.
- BeautyMatter Pro — ~$400 a year. Category deep dives.
- Combined ~$1K per year for premium trade intelligence.

**Social listening**
- Dash Hudson starter tier or equivalent — $3K to $8K a year. Monitors all five comps plus SJS across IG, TikTok, Pinterest. Feeds both the trend digest (format and claim trends emerging in social) and the teardowns (social strategy pillar).
- Alternatives: Sprout Social listening ($3K to $6K), Brandwatch entry tier ($5K to $10K).

**Amazon competitive data**
- Helium 10 Standard — $99 a month, so $1,188 a year. BSR tracking, review velocity, pricing history, variant analysis for premium beauty.
- Alternative: Jungle Scout ($149 to $349 a month).

**Launch and earned media tracking**
- LaunchMetrics Lite or Tribe Dynamics basic — $3K to $6K a year. Media impact value, creator content rollup.
- Only recommended if Soraya finds manual monitoring too time consuming after two quarters.

**Automation**
- Phantombuster — $80 a month for Instagram history scraping during pipeline back catalog build. Can cancel after initial build.
- Zapier or Make automation glue — $240 a year.

**Estimated total lightweight paid spend: $8K to $15K per year**, depending on which social listening tool and whether LaunchMetrics is added.

## Heavyweight paid layer — skip year one

Revisit post Ulta launch, Q1 2027 at earliest. Flagged here for reference.

**Mintel Beauty & Personal Care** — $20K to $40K a year. Category trends, consumer data, innovation pipeline at global scale. Best for large teams; overkill for a five brand cohort.

**Spate** — $12K to $24K a year. Search trend data for beauty. Better at the claim and ingredient pillar than Mintel; narrower than Mintel overall.

**WGSN Beauty** — $25K to $40K a year. Forward looking trend forecasting. Most valuable for pipeline planning, which is year two work for SJS.

**BeautyStreams** — $15K to $25K a year. Beauty specific trend service, younger than WGSN. Overlap with Mintel.

**LaunchMetrics full** — $15K to $40K a year. Industry standard earned media valuation.

**Tribe Dynamics (now owned by CreatorIQ)** — $12K to $25K a year. Creator and earned media tracking.

**NielsenIQ Byzzer** — $10K to $20K a year. Retail scan data; overlaps heavily with retail intel stream, not CI's lane.

**Circana (formerly NPD)** — enterprise pricing, typically $30K plus. Prestige beauty category data.

**Total heavyweight budget if deployed: $50K to $150K per year.** Not justified for year one.

## Build priorities

If the budget for year one gets cut, here's the priority order:
1. Trade press pro tiers (~$1K) — cheapest, highest reach, feeds all three sub-streams.
2. Social listening ($3K to $8K) — second highest leverage, covers digest and teardowns.
3. Amazon competitive data (~$1.2K) — cheap, specific, high value for lip pricing work.
4. Automation tools (~$300) — trivial cost.
5. LaunchMetrics Lite — only if demand proves it, quarter three onward.

## Sources by deliverable

**Monthly trend digest pulls from**: trade press, retailer new arrival pages, TikTok/IG trend signal, Credo and Provenance for sustainability, CosmeticsDesign for ingredient trends, social listening for claim patterns.

**Quarterly teardowns pull from**: brand direct (email, press room), retailer pages, Wayback for historical comparison, social listening, Amazon BSR, founder podcasts, earned media aggregators.

**Pipeline tracker pulls from**: historical Instagram via Phantombuster, press release archives, retailer "new" archives via Wayback, brand financial disclosures where public.

## Maintenance

Source list reviewed semi-annually. Drop sources that produced zero actionable signal in two quarters. Add sources flagged in trade press as emerging. Track which sources feed which signals in a simple tagged log so the source ROI is visible.
