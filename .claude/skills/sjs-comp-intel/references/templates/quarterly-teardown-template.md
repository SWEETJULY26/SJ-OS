# Quarterly Teardown Template

One doc per comp brand per quarter. Eight to twelve pages. Produced by Soraya, reviewed by Nicole, signed off by Alvin before circulation.

Direct comps (Rhode, Summer Fridays, Kosas) get the full template every quarter. Aspirational comps (LANEIGE, YTTP) can use a lighter version (sections 2, 3, 4, 8 only) in off cycles.

---

## Header block

- **Brand**: [name]
- **Tier**: Direct comp / Aspirational
- **Quarter**: Q[N] [YYYY]
- **Produced by**: [Soraya]
- **Reviewed by**: [Nicole]
- **Signed off**: [Alvin / date]

- **Change since last quarter**: [single sentence summary of the delta]

## Section 1 — Bottom line (one paragraph)

The three-sentence synthesis. What did this brand do this quarter, what does it mean for SJS, and what decision does it require from us? If this section doesn't trigger a conversation, the rest of the teardown won't either.

## Section 2 — Launches and line extensions

Every new SKU or line extension this quarter. For each:
- SKU name and one-line description
- Positioning (what benefit is it claiming)
- Price (MSRP, and promo depth if already discounted)
- Channel (DTC only, retailer exclusive, broad)
- Format and packaging notes
- Category implication (is this a bet on a trend, a defensive move, or a hero extension)

Include a count vs last four quarters to track cadence. A brand dropping from four launches per quarter to one is a signal; so is the reverse.

## Section 3 — Pricing and promo activity

Track four price points per SKU where applicable: DTC, Sephora, Ulta, Amazon. Compare against last quarter.
- Any MSRP change
- Promo depth and frequency (weeks on promo as percent of quarter)
- Bundle strategy changes
- Entry point SKU shifts (cheapest in line)
- Hero SKU price pressure (the hero's price tells you the brand's pricing power)

Cross reference the retail intel price ladder. Do not duplicate — supplement.

**Margin flag trigger**: note any pricing move that would invalidate a SJS margin floor. Route to Margin Architecture within two weeks.

## Section 4 — Packaging and formula changes

- Packaging refreshes (primary, secondary, tertiary)
- Formula reformulations
- Sustainability claim additions (refillable, recycled content, refill programs)
- Component supplier changes where publicly visible

**Margin flag trigger**: any packaging change that shifts category cost benchmarks. Route to Margin within a month.

## Section 5 — Retail footprint

- New retailer doors
- Exits
- International expansion or contraction
- Ulta specific moves (new Ulta launches, Ulta exclusive SKUs, Ulta promotional tier changes)
- Sephora/Ulta migrations (rare, high signal)

**Retail intel flag trigger**: any Ulta related signal routes to retail intel within 48 hours.

## Section 6 — Social and content strategy

- Content volume change vs last quarter (IG, TikTok, YouTube, Pinterest)
- Format mix shift (more Reels? less static? new format?)
- Creator strategy (exclusive partnerships, creator product collabs, always-on vs campaign)
- Platform emphasis shift (TikTok-first to IG-first, or vice versa)
- Organic vs paid mix if visible
- Engagement rate trend (quarter over quarter, not absolute)
- Top performing post themes this quarter

## Section 7 — Earned media and brand moments

- Notable press pickups (which publications, what framing)
- Celebrity wears and organic endorsements
- Viral moments (TikTok virality, IG moments)
- Founder media (interviews, podcast appearances, personal brand moves)
- Award wins and industry recognition
- Crisis or negative coverage (if any)

## Section 8 — What it means for SJS

This is Nicole's section. Synthesis only, not reporting.

- Which of this brand's moves map to something SJS should do, copy, avoid, or defend against?
- Where is this brand over-rotating that we can learn from without repeating?
- What does this brand's quarter tell us about the category direction?
- What is the one thing we should talk about in the next brand team meeting because of this teardown?

Recommended length: 200 to 400 words. If it's shorter than 200 words, we didn't pull enough signal. If it's longer than 400, we're padding.

## Section 9 — Cross-stream flags

Explicit list of signals routed to other streams this teardown. Format:

| Destination | Signal | Action required | Routed to | Date |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Margin | [description] | [decision needed] | [DRI name] | [date] |
| Retail Intel | [description] | [decision needed] | [DRI name] | [date] |
| C&C | [description] | [decision needed] | [DRI name] | [date] |

Every signal routed must have a corresponding entry in the signal log.

## Section 10 — What changed about the brand profile

Narrative update to the brand's baseline profile in `references/comp-brands/[brand].md`. Two to four sentences describing what shifts in how we describe this brand going forward.

Update the profile file directly when the teardown is signed off.

## Appendix — Sources and confidence

- Source list for this teardown (which of the sources in source-stack.md produced signal)
- Confidence level on each section (high / medium / low / directional)
- Known gaps — what we wanted to know and couldn't find out. Add to watch list for next quarter.

## Production notes

**Time budget per teardown**: 4 to 6 hours per direct comp, 2 to 3 hours per aspirational. Full Q1 package (three direct, two aspirational light) comes in around 20 hours including Nicole's synthesis time.

**What not to do**
- Do not reproduce retail intel's price ladder. Cross reference only.
- Do not write long on sections where nothing meaningful happened. A quarter with minor activity produces a shorter teardown. Don't pad.
- Do not skip Section 8 because it's hard. That's the entire point of the teardown.

**Formatting reminders**
- No hyphens. Em dashes or rewrite.
- Lead with bottom line (Section 1 is the bottom line).
- Benchmark against prestige skincare specifically.
- Flag confidence levels per section.
