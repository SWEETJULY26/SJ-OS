Two to three page forwardable brief. Ships by the 5th of each month. Produced by Nicole with Kate support on the social/format pillar. No Alvin sign off required — direct to Danielle and SJS brand team.

Format optimized for forwarding. If Danielle isn't forwarding it, the format is wrong.

## Section 1 — Ingredient to watch

One paragraph (100 to 150 words). The single most interesting ingredient movement this month.

Structure:
- What ingredient is gaining ground
- Where we saw it (trade press coverage, launches, social claims)
- What's driving the momentum
- Whether SJS formulas touch this ingredient now or should

**Example cue**: "Bakuchiol mentions in trade press up 40% quarter over quarter. Three of our five comps now list it on primary SKUs. Our current formulas do not feature it. Worth raising with Perrine for the Q3 PD pipeline."

## Section 2 — Format or texture trend

One paragraph (100 to 150 words). A change in how products are being built or experienced, separate from the ingredient itself.

Structure:
- What format is gaining (stick formats, tinted serums, barrier sprays, whatever)
- Who's building it
- Whether the format is new or returning
- Implication for SJS format roadmap

**Example cue**: "Stick formats continuing to migrate from color to skincare. Rhode's peptide stick, Summer Fridays' cheekbone blur stick, three indie launches. Format advantage: on-the-go ritual, single-hand use, no hygiene concerns vs dips. Our lip stick architecture is aligned; face formats not yet on roadmap."

## Section 3 — Claim pattern shift

One paragraph (100 to 150 words). What the category is starting to claim that it wasn't claiming 90 days ago. Or what it's stopping to claim.

Structure:
- What claim is emerging or receding
- Who's leading the shift
- Whether the claim is substantiated or marketing-first
- Implication for SJS claim architecture and PD pipeline

**Example cue**: "Microbiome claims hitting an inflection point. Six comp and cohort brands added microbiome-friendly language in the last 60 days. Clinical substantiation uneven — two have published studies, four are leaning on in-vitro only. Risk: claim inflation leading to retailer scrutiny. Opportunity: SJS could differentiate on clinical rigor if we invest."

## Section 4 — Sustainability or packaging move

One paragraph (100 to 150 words). A sustainability, packaging, or compliance move in the category. Often tied to retailer standards (Credo, Ulta Conscious Beauty) or regulatory shifts.

Structure:
- What happened
- Who's driving it (brand? retailer? regulator?)
- Scope — is this cosmetic or structural
- Implication for SJS

**Example cue**: "Ulta Conscious Beauty criteria refresh announced for 2027. New refillable requirement for tiered placement. Two of our comps already refillable for hero SKUs. Our Pineapple Punch and Castaway Cream jars are not yet refill-capable. Jan flagged for Q4 packaging review."

## Section 5 — What it means for SJS

Two paragraphs (200 to 300 words total). The analytical layer.

First paragraph: pattern across the four trend reads above. What's the connective tissue? Is the month pointing toward anything bigger?

Second paragraph: the one or two decisions this month's signals suggest SJS should be working toward. Not "do this now." More like "this is something Danielle and the team should be aware is building."

This section is the entire reason the digest exists. It is not optional. It cannot be delegated to a junior writer. Nicole writes this.

## Production notes

**Time budget**: 6 to 8 hours across Nicole (4-5) and Kate (2-3). Kate does source scanning and initial surfacing; Nicole writes.

**Format discipline**
- Never exceed three pages. If it doesn't fit, the analysis is lazy.
- Never lead with sourcing ("we scanned 80 articles this month"). Lead with the ingredient, format, claim, or move.
- Never cite more than one source per trend read in the body. Keep it forwardable.

**What this digest is not**
- Not a news roundup. We are not summarizing the month's beauty news.
- Not a competitor report. Teardowns do that.
- Not opinion. Every trend read must have a source or multiple comps demonstrating it.

**If a month is quiet**
A quiet month is a valid month. Ship a shorter digest rather than padding. "Nothing material shifted in [pillar] this month" is a legitimate read. Pad once and the digest loses its forwardable quality permanently.

**Formatting reminders**
- No hyphens. Em dashes or rewrite.
- Benchmark against prestige skincare specifically.
- Flag confidence levels inline if any read is directional.
- Apply Sweet July Skin brand guidelines to the PDF version before distribution (Canva template referenced in sweet-july-skin-brand skill).
