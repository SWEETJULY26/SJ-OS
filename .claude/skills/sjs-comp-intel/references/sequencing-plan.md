# Sequencing Plan

The recommended build order. Designed to produce first deliverables within 30 days, quarterly rhythm by month four, and pipeline tracking without a parallel workstream for it.

## Core principle

Run sub-stream 3 (monthly trend digest) and sub-stream 1 (quarterly teardowns) in parallel because they share roughly 70% of sources. Let sub-stream 2 (pipeline tracking) emerge as a rollup of teardown data over time rather than building it as a separate effort.

## Month 1

**Stand up infrastructure.**
- Confirm tool stack and kick off procurement (Glossy Pro, BeautyMatter Pro, social listening trial). Ciarra handles the procurement paperwork.
- Set up shared working folder (Notion page or SharePoint) for CI deliverables and signal log.
- Populate the Rhode comp profile in full using retail intel's existing profile plus two weeks of current monitoring (Soraya + Kate). Already scaffolded in references, so this is population work not template building.
- Skeleton populate the other four comp profiles (Summer Fridays, Kosas, LANEIGE, YTTP) with basics: positioning one-liner, current price ladder snapshot, recent launch list, Ulta/Sephora presence map. Full populations happen during first quarterly teardowns.
- Build source subscriber accounts and monitoring dashboards for all lightweight paid tools.
- Start drafting first trend digest in parallel. Target delivery: day 30.

**Deliverables end of month 1**
- Tool stack live and billed.
- Five brand profiles in shared folder (Rhode full, others skeleton).
- First trend digest drafted (not yet delivered — needs one more cycle).
- Quarterly teardown template field-tested against Rhode data.

## Month 2

**First digest ships. Teardown production starts.**
- Finalize and ship first monthly trend digest on or before the 5th. Danielle and SJS brand team receive. Flag any cross-stream signals discovered.
- Start first quarterly teardown production. Begin with Rhode since profile is fully populated. One brand per two weeks.
- Nicole holds first monthly 15 minute check in with each other stream DRI. Establishes the handoff rhythm.
- Soraya refines the teardown template based on Rhode production learnings.

**Deliverables end of month 2**
- First monthly digest delivered.
- Rhode quarterly teardown complete.
- Template refinements locked.

## Month 3

**Second digest ships. Teardown production continues.**
- Second monthly trend digest by the 5th.
- Summer Fridays and Kosas teardowns completed this month.
- LANEIGE and YTTP profiles fully populated during teardown production (they don't need full teardowns this cycle since they're aspirational — lighter format appropriate).
- Cross-stream signal log has first quarter of entries. Nicole reviews for handoff quality.

**Deliverables end of month 3**
- Second monthly digest delivered.
- Three full teardowns complete (Rhode, Summer Fridays, Kosas).
- Two aspirational profiles populated with lighter read.

## Month 4

**First full quarterly teardown delivered. Monthly rhythm established.**
- Third monthly trend digest by the 5th.
- First full quarterly teardown package delivered — all five brands, synthesis memo, cross-stream flags routed. This is the first time Danielle and the brand team see the full CI output.
- Alvin signs off before it circulates.

**Deliverables end of month 4**
- Third monthly digest delivered.
- Complete Q1 quarterly teardown package delivered.
- Handoff rules pressure tested, revised if needed.

## Months 5 through 6

**Rhythm. Pipeline tracker preparation.**
- Monthly digest rhythm continues.
- Prep for pipeline tracker back catalog build: scope the analyst contractor hire ($3K to $6K one time), define the dataset specification, identify the tool (Phantombuster for Instagram scraping).
- Decision point: month 5 retrospective. What's working in the stream? What's not? Adjust tool stack, cadence, or cohort before Q2 teardown starts.

## Month 7

**Q2 quarterly teardown. Pipeline back catalog kicks off.**
- Q2 teardown produced against month 4 baseline. By now the standing template is mature and production time is halved.
- Back catalog build starts: three years of launch history for all five brands, structured dataset.
- Monthly digest continues.

## Month 10

**Q3 teardown. Back catalog complete.**
- Q3 teardown produced.
- Back catalog build delivered. First pipeline tracker view published as addendum to Q3 teardown.
- Pipeline tracker becomes a standing Q1 refresh thereafter.

## Month 12 and beyond

**Stream is mature.**
- Monthly digest, quarterly teardowns, annual pipeline refresh.
- Post Ulta launch reality check: which of the year one predictions held, which didn't. Adjust watch list if needed.
- Heavyweight tooling decision window opens — revisit Mintel, Spate, WGSN based on year one ROI signal.

## Stage gates

Three points where the stream gets reassessed before continuing to the next phase:

**Gate 1 (end of month 2)**: first digest shipped. Question: is the format actually forwardable and useful? If Danielle isn't forwarding it, the format is wrong.

**Gate 2 (end of month 4)**: first full quarterly teardown shipped. Question: did any teardown produce a decision? If no decisions were triggered by the teardown package, the depth is wrong.

**Gate 3 (end of month 12)**: full year review. Question: ROI of each tool, each source, each sub-stream. Cut what isn't producing signal, deepen what is.

## Effort estimates

**Month 1 stand up**: roughly 40 hours across the team (Nicole 15, Soraya 15, Kate 8, Ciarra 2).

**Ongoing monthly effort**: roughly 12 hours (Nicole 5, Kate 4, Soraya 3). About a day and a half of team time.

**Quarterly teardown month**: add roughly 20 hours that month (Soraya 12, Kate 5, Nicole 3). About two and a half days of team time in the teardown production month.

**Pipeline back catalog (month 7 to 10)**: 40 to 60 hours if internal, or $3K to $6K and ~15 hours of oversight if externalized.

**Total year one team time**: roughly 200 hours across the team. Less than one full FTE month when distributed across three people.
