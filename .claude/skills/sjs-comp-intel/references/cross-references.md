# Cross-Stream Handoff Rules

Competitive Intelligence is the connective tissue across the five research streams. Most of its value compounds through handoffs. These are the standing rules.

## To Margin Architecture

**What gets flagged**
- Any competitor pricing move (MSRP change, sustained promo depth over 20%, bundle restructuring) that would invalidate a margin floor in the SJS architecture.
- Competitor packaging refreshes that shift category cost benchmarks — e.g., if three of the five comps move to refillable glass, that changes the composition rule baseline.
- Competitor formula reformulations that suggest ingredient cost is a category pressure (e.g., multiple comps dropping retinol in favor of bakuchiol citing cost of goods as reason).
- New comp SKUs launching below SJS price floors — especially in lip, where the acquisition strategy is sensitive to competitor moves.

**Cadence**
- Pricing signals: within two weeks of detection.
- Packaging and formula signals: monthly aggregation.
- Hard margin floor invalidation signals: same day.

**Exchange format**
Short memo, three paragraphs: what we saw, what it means for the margin framework, what decision is required. Route to the Margin Architecture chat for review and integration.

**What Margin sends back to CI**
- Which competitor moves Margin wants CI to watch for specifically (e.g., "flag any LANEIGE lip pricing change within 48 hours").
- Updated floor values that change what "invalidation" means.

## To Retail Performance Intelligence

**Standing boundaries**
- Price ladder data lives in retail intel. CI does not rebuild it. CI consumes retail intel's price ladder as authoritative.
- The retail intel 10 brand cohort is the canonical comp universe. CI's five brand deep watch is a deliberate subset. Profiles should be unified, not duplicated.
- Promo calendar is jointly maintained but retail intel owns the structure. CI contributes monthly pricing surveillance.

**What gets flagged**
- Competitor retail footprint shifts (new retailers, exits, international expansion) — immediate flag to retail intel for inclusion in launch benchmarking.
- Ulta specific moves by any comp (new Ulta launches, Ulta exclusive SKUs, Ulta promotional participation) — immediate flag given SJS fall 2026 Ulta launch.
- Sephora to Ulta migrations or vice versa — rare but high signal for the launch strategy.

**Cadence**
- Ulta related signals: within 48 hours.
- Other retailer signals: within two weeks.
- Monthly: CI ships a pricing delta memo to retail intel for promo calendar input.

**What retail intel sends back to CI**
- Updates to the canonical cohort definition.
- Comp profile updates that CI should mirror in its deep watch files.
- Sell-through velocity signals that inform which comp moves are working (and therefore worth watching more closely).

## To Consumer & Category Research

**The claims and whitespace loop**
- CI's trend digest feeds C&C's whitespace analysis. When CI sees every comp moving toward a claim pattern (e.g., "microbiome friendly"), C&C validates whether consumers are actually buying that claim or it's industry noise.
- Claims direction flows CI to C&C, not the other way. CI reports what competitors are claiming; C&C tests whether those claims resonate.
- When C&C identifies a consumer gap, CI validates whether competitors are already addressing it — determining whether the gap is a real opportunity or unserved demand that the market has decided is unprofitable.

**What gets flagged**
- Emerging claim pattern across three or more comps: route to C&C for consumer validation.
- Ingredient trend consensus in trade press: route to C&C for consumer resonance test.
- Sustainability moves by two or more comps: route to C&C for brand team priority review.

**Cadence**
- Monthly, via trend digest. C&C reads the digest as primary input each month.
- Quarterly synthesis meeting (suggested): Nicole plus the C&C DRI, 30 minutes, review the quarter's signals.

**What C&C sends back to CI**
- Consumer validated priorities that CI should weight more heavily in the trend digest.
- Whitespace hypotheses that need competitive validation — "is anyone serving this gap, and if so, how."

## To Operational Resilience

Lower frequency but non-zero.

**What gets flagged**
- Competitor supply chain disruptions visible in trade press (e.g., a comp's manufacturer fire, raw material allocation issue, chargeback dispute with retailer). Signals category risk.
- Competitor 3PL or fulfillment changes — rare, high signal when visible.
- Clean standards shifts by Credo, EWG, or Ulta that any comp responds to — category compliance indicator.

**Cadence**
- As it surfaces, no standing rhythm needed.

## Inter-stream meetings

Recommended, not required:
- **Monthly 15 minute check-in**: Nicole (CI) with each of the other four stream DRIs. Quick signal exchange, not a working session.
- **Quarterly 60 minute synthesis**: all five stream DRIs plus Alvin. Pattern recognition across streams.

## Signal logging

Every flagged cross-stream signal gets logged in a shared Notion page or SharePoint doc with:
- Date detected
- Source brand (if applicable)
- Signal type
- Destination stream
- Action taken / decision made / status
- Who owns follow-up

This log is the audit trail for whether the cross-stream handoffs are actually producing decisions or just moving paper. Review quarterly. If more than 30% of signals have no "action taken" entry after a quarter, the stream isn't using CI effectively and we rework the handoff rules.
