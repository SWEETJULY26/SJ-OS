## Cross-channel pricing observations

1. **Hero SKU price parity across channels** — Lip Sleeping Mask is $24 at DTC, Sephora, Target, Walmart, and Amazon. This unified price floor protects the brand at mass without cannibalizing Sephora prestige positioning. The product's success is partly because it survives the same price test in every store the consumer might encounter it.
2. **Off-price leakage at TJ Maxx** — LANEIGE confirmed at TJ Maxx; this is older inventory or seasonal close-out and is brand-tolerated rather than channel-encouraged. Material risk: any LANEIGE customer can plausibly find Lip Sleeping Mask <$24 at TJX, weakening the $24 price point.
3. **TikTok Shop disruption** — flash-deal pricing ($17.98 LSM, 9-for-$21 holiday bundle) creates real price confusion. Consumer expectation for LSM has been dragged below $24 by these episodic moments. SJS launching at UBM should not assume that the Sephora-listed price is what the consumer's "real" price expectation is.
4. **No Ulta channel** — full column blank. The most viral lip-treatment brand in the US has zero Ulta footprint. SJS UBM launch faces less direct lip competition than expected.
