## Cross-channel pricing discipline

- **Uniform across DTC and Sephora US:** Yes, for every hero SKU verified. Summer Fridays maintains strict channel parity — no channel-exclusive pricing observed.
- **Discount mechanisms:**
  - Auto-Replenish on Sephora: 5% off (Jet Lag Mask $49 → $46.55; Lip Butter Balm $24 → $22.80)
  - BNPL: Klarna / Afterpay / PayPal — 4 interest-free installments on every hero SKU (Jet Lag Mask $12.25 × 4, Lip Butter Balm $6.00 × 4)
  - DTC subscribe-and-save: 15% off + free shipping for recurring orders (brand's only sitewide discount mechanic)
  - Recycling empties program: 5 empties → $25 DTC gift card
  - Sephora Biannual Spring/Holiday Savings Event: 10% Insiders / 15% VIB / 20% Rouge — **Summer Fridays was flagged as a featured brand in April 2026's event.**
- **Channel-exclusive pricing:** None observed. Bundles and value sets may be Sephora-exclusive or DTC-exclusive formats, but hero SKU pricing is uniform.

## Price architecture insights

1. **Flat hero pricing at $49, lip gateway at $24.** Summer Fridays anchors the accessible-prestige band with a clean two-tier architecture — $24 lip for discovery, $45–$49 hero tier for commitment. The $24–$49 spread is the structural zone this cohort defends.
2. **Mini-size strategy is load-bearing.** The Jet Lag Mask mini at ~$25 creates a price-parity entry point with the Lip Butter Balm, widening the accessible top-of-funnel without eroding the $49 hero price. SJS should consider mini-size architecture for its own heroes as a gateway play.
3. **Fragrance at $82 marks the upper ceiling.** Sunlit Vanilla (March 2026) stretches the ladder into premium territory, but it's a category halo move, not a repricing signal for skincare. Skincare heroes stay anchored at $45–$49.
4. **Pricing discipline + participation in Sephora's calendar.** Summer Fridays doesn't discount DTC sitewide but does participate in Sephora's two major Savings Events (Spring + Holiday). This is the "promotional rhythm" playbook — protect DTC price integrity, show up in Sephora's demand peaks.
5. **9+ shades of Lip Butter Balm at uniform $24.** Shade proliferation is the lip strategy, not price proliferation. Every color commands the same ticket — signals that formula/benefit is the value prop, shade is the personalization.

## SJS positioning implication

1. **$24–$49 is the defended accessible-prestige zone.** Summer Fridays, Rhode ($20–$32), and the entry tier of Tatcha ($19–$29) collectively define the floor-to-ceiling range SJS needs to price into. SJS's current lip line at $20 is at the **Rhode anchor**, not the Summer Fridays anchor — a deliberate step-down from the broader peer band. This is a positioning choice, not an oversight, but worth surfacing explicitly in the margin architecture work.
2. **Sephora-exclusive US specialty is a structural advantage for SJS at UBM.** No head-to-head shelf competition from Summer Fridays on Ulta.com. If SJS wins organic search for terms Summer Fridays dominates at Sephora ("overnight mask," "tinted lip balm"), that's pure whitespace capture.
3. **Mini-size hero strategy deserves a feasibility check.** If SJS launches a ~$45–49 full-size hero and a ~$22–25 mini version, the entry-to-hero architecture mirrors the Summer Fridays ladder and gives UBM algorithm two price-point entry points to surface in search. **This is a specific tactical recommendation worth raising with PD.**
4. **Sephora Savings Event participation is the promotional norm, not the exception.** Pricing discipline + twice-yearly 10/15/20% tiered discounts. UBM promo participation should follow the same posture — show up in 2–3 marquee events per year, not every weekly email.
5. **Fragrance at $82 is the ceiling the cohort is pushing.** If/when SJS considers a fragrance extension (ritual-hero brands often do), $82 is the peer-anchored price.
