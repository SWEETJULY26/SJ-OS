## Cross-channel pricing discipline

- **Uniform across Ulta and expected Sephora/DTC:** Tatcha's historical pattern has been strict cross-channel parity. Needs verification `[TK]` on next pull but confidence: High.
- **Discount mechanisms (Ulta):**
  - Ulta Rewards points — 1 pt/$1 on all Tatcha purchases
  - Ulta Rewards Credit Card — 2 pts/$1 + 20% off first purchase
  - Tatcha participates in Ulta "21 Days of Beauty" promotional event (observed 2025)
  - Ulta Spring Haul / Fall Haul tiered promotions — Tatcha has participated
  - Only-at-Ulta value sets delivering 25–42% off component MSRP (the deepest price discipline breach — but contained within gifting/bundle format)
- **Discount mechanisms (Sephora):**
  - Auto-Replenish 5% `[TK verify for Tatcha]`
  - Participation in biannual Sephora Savings Event (10/15/20% tiered)
- **Discount mechanisms (DTC):**
  - Sample program with every order
  - Birthday gift + welcome offer
  - Occasional Friends & Family event
- **Channel-exclusive pricing:** None at the hero-SKU level. Exclusivity is in **product form** (Only at Ulta SKUs + value sets) rather than price.
- **Kit / bundle math:** Tatcha's exclusive Ulta sets range from 25% off (Trio) to 42% off (Little Luxuries) the implied component MSRP. **This is significantly deeper discounting than the brand accepts anywhere else** — Ulta exclusives are the one place Tatcha is willing to compromise price integrity to drive trial + AOV.

## Price architecture insights

1. **The 3-size ladder on heroes is the single biggest insight.** Dewy Skin Cream at $25/$45/$93 is not an accident — it's a fundamental architecture. The mini anchors the entry band, the standard is the margin-dense middle, the jumbo is the loyalty-refill/gifting premium. **Review counts aggregate across all sizes on one Ulta PDP**, compounding SEO + social proof.
2. **10 of 30 SKUs are priced under $25.** The sub-$25 floor is load-bearing — it's how Ulta shoppers find Tatcha without a $65 commit. Tatcha's commitment to mini SKUs as a first-class product category (not just sampling) is the architectural signature.
3. **Only one SKU at $125.** The Silk Cream is the apex ceiling; everything else lives below $100. **This matches the Goop pattern where the apex tier is lightly populated.** $125 feels like the true prestige-skincare-at-Ulta ceiling — anything above is Sephora-exclusive territory.
4. **Lip is priced $28–$40, higher than the Sephora-exclusive cohort.** Melting Lip Balm $29, Kissu Plumping Lip Mask $29, Kissu Lip Tint $34, Kissu Lip Treatment $40. **Tatcha's lip is priced above Summer Fridays ($24) and Rhode ($20)** — signaling a premium positioning that leans on the ritual / heritage brand equity.
5. **Only-at-Ulta bundle discounts are steep (25–42% off).** This is the one area where Tatcha's cross-channel price discipline breaks — bundles are where deep discounting is allowed because the format itself is protected (not the SKU). **Useful pattern for any brand negotiating retail entry: offer bundle exclusives at steep discounts without eroding hero SKU prices.**

## SJS positioning implication

1. **Tatcha's multi-size architecture is the playbook SJS should follow.** For every hero SJS launches on UBM, the default assumption should be a 2- or 3-size family (mini ~$20, standard ~$40–$50, jumbo ~$85+). This compounds reviews on one PDP, expands the shopper funnel, and captures gifting/travel occasions. **Add to PD roadmap consideration.**
2. **The sub-$30 entry band is structurally load-bearing for Ulta shoppers.** Tatcha has 10 SKUs here. SJS currently has strong coverage in this band through the lip line ($20s) but is thin on cleansers, mists, serums at the entry price. UBM launch readiness should include at least 3–5 SKUs under $30 as discovery anchors.
3. **$29 is the peer-anchored premium lip price.** Both the Tatcha Melting Lip Balm ($29) and Kissu Plumping Lip Mask ($29) sit at this anchor. Combined with Summer Fridays Lip Butter Balm at $24, the defended premium-lip zone is $24–$29. **SJS lip line currently at $20 is pricing below the premium zone — this is the repricing question from the margin architecture work, validated by a second cohort peer.**
4. **Tatcha's exclusive Ulta lip launch is a strategic playbook SJS could eventually mirror.** When/if SJS moves to main-line Ulta, offering a launch-exclusive SKU (say, a Sorrel-shade tinted lip mask only at Ulta for the first 6 months) is a well-trodden deal term.
5. **The Silk Sunscreen data (3.9★ despite 790 reviews) is the sunscreen-category risk illustrated.** SPF is a high-complaint SKU category. SJS has no SPF in PD and no plans — and the Tatcha data reinforces why that's the right call at launch. Entering SPF pre-brand-trust is a star-rating risk that can drag down adjacent hero SKU visibility on Ulta's algorithm.
6. **Bundle/set pricing discipline matters.** Tatcha's 25–42% bundle discounts are a **ceiling**, not a starting point. If SJS launches sets on UBM, anchor at 15–25% off component MSRP to start — bundles that discount deeper train shoppers to wait for them and cannibalize single-SKU AOV.
