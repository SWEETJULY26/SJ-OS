## Pricing pattern analysis

**The Topicals price ladder sits in the specialty accessible-prestige zone:**

| Price point | Example SKU | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| ~$15-$24 (body + limited drops) | Body Bar 2-pack, Eye Masks | Entry + secondary category |
| $28-$30 | High Roller Ingrown Tonic, Faded Mist | Mid-tier specialty |
| **$32–$38** | **Like Butter Mask $32 → Moisturizer $34 → Faded Serum $38** | **Core hero zone** |
| $60+ (kits) | Faded Starter Kit, Acne Reset Kit | Value-aggregated kits for education-attached funnel |

**Topicals' $32-$38 core range is exactly where SJS should benchmark UBM skincare pricing.** Above Naturium ($19-$25) and Byoma ($14-$20) caps; below Summer Fridays ($45-$49) and Rhode ($35-$42) hero tier; parity with Glow Recipe Dew Drops ($36) and Plum Plump Cream ($34).

## Promo / discount mechanics

- **Sephora Savings Event** participation (10/15/20% by Beauty Insider tier, biannual)
- **Sephora Auto-Replenish** 5% off on hero SKUs (Faded $36.10 / Like Butter Moisturizer $32.30 with auto-replenish)
- **No standalone DTC sitewide discounts** observed
- **Limited-edition drops** (Cognac eye mask color set, limited-edition bundles) create scarcity storytelling, not price erosion
- **Topicals Certified esthetician program** (Oct 2025) is a value-add freebie attached to Faded Starter Kit purchase — education-as-promo rather than discount-as-promo

**Read:** Most disciplined pricing posture in the cohort alongside Glow Recipe and Summer Fridays. Faded moved $36→$38 in 5 years (+5.5%), well under category inflation. Heroes hold their price; bundles deliver aggregated value. Olowe explicit: DTC is "test and learn" + community channel, not discount channel.

## Cross-channel pricing observations

1. **Sephora-DTC-Amazon parity** on Faded ($38) and Like Butter Moisturizer ($34). Three-channel parity is rigorously maintained.
2. **No Ulta column** — Topicals is intentionally Sephora-exclusive in US specialty. Multiple founder interviews reference this as a deliberate choice tied to Sephora Accelerate incubator origins.
3. **Only 5.5% price increase on Faded over 5 years** — the most disciplined price hold in the cohort. Industry norm over same period was 15-25% cumulative increase. This is a deliberate brand-health investment.
4. **Revenue mix is roughly 1/3 each across Sephora / Amazon / DTC** (per Olowe Sept 2025) — the most diversified channel-revenue structure in the cohort. Most other brands are 60-80% Sephora-dependent.
5. **Premium pricing but tight SKU count** — only ~15-20 SKUs, 2 of which carry majority of brand awareness. Opposite architecture from Naturium (54 SKUs) or Byoma (39 SKUs) who spread bets wide at lower prices. Topicals concentrates bets narrow at higher prices.
6. **The $34 vs $32 Like Butter split is a pricing experiment worth studying** — same hero formula name, two SKUs at adjacent price points ($2 gap), differentiated by use-case (mask vs. moisturizer). The brand is using minor price points to segment use cases within a single franchise.
