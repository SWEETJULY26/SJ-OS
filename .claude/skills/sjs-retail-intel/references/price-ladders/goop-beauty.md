## Cross-channel pricing discipline

- **In-store vs. online-only bifurcation at Ulta:** Only 4 of 10 SKUs ($38–$68) placed in-store. All 6 SKUs at $95+ are online-only. This is the core architectural observation. Ulta's merchants are rationing shelf space to the productivity tier.
- **Discount mechanisms (Ulta):**
  - Ulta Rewards points on all purchases
  - Ulta Rewards Credit Card: 2 pts/$1 + 20% off first purchase
  - 50% off select skincare promotions (Goop participation still emerging — no observed Goop-specific markdowns in Spring 2026 Ulta events)
  - BNPL offered but not prominent
- **Discount mechanisms (DTC, goop.com):** Periodic Friends & Family (~20% sitewide, quarterly cadence), end-of-season clearance, "Beauty Pack" curated subscription boxes
- **Channel-exclusive pricing:** Not directly observable without parallel DTC/Sephora pull. Goop's historical pattern has been cross-channel parity, which is the default expectation.

## Price architecture insights

1. **Wide ladder from $35 to $150 — the steepest in the cohort.** Goop spans 4.3x entry-to-apex, significantly wider than Summer Fridays (~3.4x entry-to-apex after fragrance) or Rhode (1.6x, lip-to-skincare). The breadth is a Goop-specific choice that mirrors its "one-stop prestige routine" positioning.
2. **The $95+ tier lives almost exclusively online at Ulta.** This is the clearest working example in the cohort of a retailer segmenting SKUs by velocity expectation. Ulta puts accessible prestige on the shelf, parks premium online where the shelf-productivity bar is lower.
3. **NAD+ Peptide Rich Cream ($105) as the newest launch.** Goop is doubling down on the peptide trend via a new NAD+ formulation at premium pricing — but launching it online-only at Ulta. Signals Goop isn't willing to bet physical shelf space on an unproven new SKU even with Paltrow's platform.
4. **Pricing compression at Ulta reveals the "prestige-acceptable" band.** The Ulta in-store assortment ($38–$68) matches almost exactly the Summer Fridays in-Sephora price architecture ($24–$49, plus fragrance at $82). Different retailers are converging on a "shelf-acceptable" prestige skincare band of roughly $35–$70. **This is the zone SJS is explicitly designing toward.**

## SJS positioning implication

1. **The Goop in-store ladder ($38–$68) is the closest cohort price match to SJS's target hero tier.** If SJS hero lands at $42–$52, it sits directly alongside Goop's Microderm Exfoliator ($42) and bridges toward Goop's Water-Cream ($68). Cohort-validated zone.
2. **Online-only SKU strategy at Ulta is a real option for SJS's premium extensions.** If SJS ever develops a $75–$125 apex SKU (peptide-rich, ritual-hero anchor), launching it online-only at UBM while keeping mainstream SKUs at $30–$55 is a proven pattern — Goop is literally doing this now.
3. **Goop's struggle with the 72-Hour Water-Cream is the most important cautionary data point in the cohort.** 8 months in, 7 reviews, 3.3 stars — despite being an in-store endcap at 800 Ulta doors. This is what "slow velocity at launch" looks like for a premium moisturizer on Ulta. SJS's UBM velocity modeling should bake in this as a plausible base-case for a $68+ SKU. **Key unknown: is this a Goop-specific problem (product quality, brand trust erosion) or a structural channel problem (Ulta shoppers don't commit to $68 moisturizers from new brands).** Worth monitoring quarterly.
4. **Discount posture at Ulta requires active decision.** Ulta runs 50% off skincare events regularly, but Goop has not visibly participated at that depth. SJS needs a posture decision on UBM: participate deeply in Ulta's promo calendar (velocity play, margin erosion) or hold discipline like Goop (slower velocity, cleaner price integrity).
