## Price architecture insights

1. **Flat hero pricing at $32.** Both skincare heroes (Peptide Glazing Fluid, Barrier Restore Cream) sit at the same price point — creates a clear "Rhode hero = $32" mental anchor. Helps routine-building and cross-sell math.
2. **Lip at $20 is the gateway, full stop.** Entry point is deliberately accessible. Three lip SKUs in the $20–$23 band drive the volume (2,300 + 1,000 + 430 = ~3,700 reviews combined at 7.5mo vs. ~525 for the two skincare heroes).
3. **No step-down (sub-$20) SKU.** There is no discovery SKU below $20. The floor is $20, and everything else builds upward. This is tighter than most peer brands.
4. **Pricing discipline is the strategy.** Rhode does not promote its way into demand — no sitewide discounting, no GWP, no flash sales observed on DTC. The 5% Auto-Replenish on Sephora is the only programmatic discount, and it's retailer-mechanics rather than brand-initiated.

---

## SJS positioning implication

1. **$32 hero is the founder-led premium-accessible anchor.** This is the price other aesthetic-forward founder brands benchmark to. SJS hero pricing vs. this anchor is the most important cross-brand positioning question.
2. **If SJS wants lip as a gateway, $20 is the peer-anchored price.** Going below ($16–$18) signals mass / differentiated; going above ($24+) signals premium / prestige. Either is a real choice — just know Rhode set the anchor.
3. **Pricing discipline enables this price tier.** If SJS launches at Rhode-tier prices on UBM but runs regular % off or GWP to drive velocity, we erode the positioning. UBM promo posture needs to be decided deliberately, not opportunistically.
4. **No sub-$20 SKU is a gap we could fill.** Rhode leaves the under-$20 discovery space wide open. That's a positioning option for SJS (entry disruptor) — or a signal we should leave it alone and compete in the $20+ premium tier.
