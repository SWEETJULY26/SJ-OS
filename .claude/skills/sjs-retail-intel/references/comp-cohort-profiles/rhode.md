# Rhode

**Last refreshed:** 2026-04-20
**Refresh priority:** High
**Cohort tier:** 1 — Closest structural peer
**Why in cohort:** Founder-led celebrity skincare that scaled DTC-first, then did a single mega-retail launch. Closest positioning analog to SJS: aesthetic-forward, minimalist, accessible-premium pricing, founder is primary marketing engine. Structural/positioning benchmark — **not a shelf competitor on UBM**.

---

## Snapshot

- **Founded:** 2022 by Hailey Bieber
- **Positioning:** "Glazed donut skin" — minimalist, barrier-supporting skincare, curated tight assortment
- **Ownership:** Acquired by e.l.f. Beauty; announced May 2025, closed August 2025. Up to $1B ($800M close + $200M earnout). Bieber = CCO + Head of Innovation
- **Revenue:** $212M net sales (12 months ending March 2025, DTC-only). e.l.f. guided FY2026 to $260–265M (up from $200M guide post-Sephora launch) — implying ~70% YoY growth

## Product architecture

- **SKU count:** ~15–20 across skincare + lip + blush (intentionally tight)
- **Hero SKUs:**
  - **Peptide Lip Treatment** — cultural anchor, gateway / entry SKU
  - **Peptide Glazing Fluid** — skincare hero (had 100K+ waitlist pre-Sephora)
- **Category mix:** Skincare (cleansers, serums, moisturizers, mists) + Lip (treatments, tints, masks, plumpers) + Blush (Pocket Blush). Lip drives volume; skincare drives margin/positioning.
- **Bundle strategy:** Not traditional bundles — instead uses limited-edition drops and collabs (Rhode x The Biebers, holiday sets) as the cultural bundle play.

## Launch benchmarks — Sephora US/Canada debut (Sept 4, 2025)

**Day 1–2:**
- **$10M in sales in first 2 days** (YipitData / WWD / Glossy)
- **~40% of Sephora's total sales** on launch day
- **~3 Rhode items sold per second**
- **60% existing Sephora shoppers / 40% redirected** from Ulta, Target, rhodeskin.com

**Quarter 1 post-launch (Oct–Dec 2025):**
- **$128M contribution** to e.l.f. Beauty (first full quarter post-Sephora)

## Marketing posture

- **Founder amplification:** Maximum. Bieber IS the marketing — every launch telegraphed through her personal channels first.
- **Signature moves:** Viral packaging (bath bomb mailers, Lip Case as phone accessory, ice-dunk), Coachella activations (invite-only, 2024–2026), celebrity/model ambassadors (Schiffer, Tookes, Consani), husband-as-ambassador (Rhode x The Biebers 2026), cultural coinage ("glazed donut skin").
- **Earned >> paid.** Relies on Bieber organic reach + tight PR seeding. Ranks #1 or #2 in Traackr creator-marketing (Feb 2026).
- **Promo posture:** Minimal DTC sitewide discounting. No traditional GWP. Uses drops and collabs instead. Preserves pricing discipline — Auto-Replenish 5% on Sephora is the main programmatic discount observed.

## SJS implications

1. **Rhode's Sephora launch pulled ~40% of spend from Ulta + Target + DTC** — peer brand prestige launches create UBM headwinds even when the comp isn't on our shelf. Track upcoming Sephora prestige launches against SJS launch/milestone calendar.
2. **Lip as entry, skincare as premium.** Rhode's review-volume pattern (lip ~4–5× skincare) validates lip products as the gateway SKU strategy. Worth pressure-testing SJS's assortment to ensure we have a true accessible-entry hero that drives discovery.
3. **Founder amplification is the highest-leverage variable.** Rhode's model is built on Bieber's organic reach. Ayesha Curry's reach + Sweet July cross-audience is the nearest structural analog in our cohort — model carefully.
4. **Tight assortment + cultural drops.** Rhode launched with ~10 SKUs and grows through drops, not line extensions. SJS UBM launch should not over-extend on SKU count at launch.
