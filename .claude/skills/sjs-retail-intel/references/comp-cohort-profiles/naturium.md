# Naturium

**Last refreshed:** 2026-04-20
**Refresh priority:** Critical
**Cohort tier:** 3 — Accessible clean down-price anchor
**Why in cohort:** **The most direct UBM/Ulta shelf competitor for SJS in the cohort.** E.l.f. Beauty-owned skincare brand available in all 1,400 Ulta doors + ulta.com since July 14, 2024. 54 SKUs at Ulta spanning face, body, and lip at $10-$25 price points — sits squarely in the zone SJS is targeting. Also a preview of how E.l.f.'s playbook will be applied to Rhode (acquired 2025) and by extension reshape the accessible-prestige shelf through 2027.

---

## Snapshot

- **Founded:** 2019 by brand accelerator **The Center**. **Susan Yara** (former beauty journalist; NewBeauty alumna; YouTube skincare creator with large organic following) joined as brand Founder in 2020.
- **Positioning:** "Clinically effective yet accessible" — biocompatible formulations anchored in active ingredients + botanicals. Ingredient-led, science-forward, accessible-clean. No premium-prestige aspiration — explicitly the "masstige disruptor" at the bottom of the prestige stack.
- **Ownership:** **Acquired by E.l.f. Beauty (NYSE: ELF) October 2023 for $355M** (cash + stock) — E.l.f.'s first M&A deal since 2020 (pre-pandemic acquisition of W3ll People for $27M). E.l.f. portfolio now: e.l.f. Cosmetics, e.l.f. Skin, Keys Soulcare, Well People, Naturium, and rhode (rhode acquired 2025).
- **Revenue:** Not separately disclosed in E.l.f. reporting. Signal: E.l.f. Beauty surpassed $1B net sales (FY2024), with "21 consecutive quarters of growth" per CEO Tarang Amin May 2024. Naturium's Ulta expansion announced at FY2024 Q4 call as part of E.l.f.'s strategy to close its skincare category share gap: "As compared to the 10.5% share we have in cosmetics, we have less than 2% share in skincare." The Glow Getter Body Wash has sold over 1M units.

## Launch benchmarks — Ulta debut (July 14, 2024, 1,400 stores)

**Context:** E.l.f. acquired Naturium October 2023 → Ulta expansion announced May 2024 (Q4 earnings call) → launched July 14, 2024 across all 1,400 Ulta stores + ulta.com simultaneously.

- **Assortment:** 45 SKUs at launch, expanded to 54 SKUs by April 2026 (20% SKU growth in ~21 months)
- **Placement:** Full main-line Ulta shelf (not UBM, not endcap-only)
- **Ulta-exclusive launches:** Glow Getter Body Oil, Barrier Bounce, Vitamin Bright
- **Ulta review velocity at 21 months (2026-04-20, top SKUs):** Plant Ceramide Cream 462 reviews / Intense Overnight Sleeping Cream 381 / Purple Ginseng Balm 361 / Ultra-Lightweight Face Fluid SPF 50 336 reviews. **~200–460 reviews per hero SKU at ~21 months**.
- **Trade press:** Ulta SVP Merchandising Penny Coy called it "viral, accessible and efficacious" at launch — same language E.l.f. uses internally. Retail Dive framed it as "Naturium's biggest wholesale opportunity."
- **Pop-up:** First-ever Naturium LA pop-up October 2025 (post-Ulta-expansion brand activation).

**Comparison context:** Naturium at 21 months in Ulta has already exceeded Goop Beauty's 8-month Ulta review velocity by 10-60x on equivalent SKUs. The gap is partly time (Naturium has been there 2.5x longer) but mostly positioning — Naturium at $20-$25 turns at Ulta-scale velocity; Goop at $68 does not.

## Marketing posture

- **Founder amplification:** High. Susan Yara has genuine organic beauty-creator authority (NewBeauty background + large YouTube following pre-Naturium). She is the brand voice in PR, DTC content, and launch activations.
- **Signature moves:** Ingredient-led PDP copy (every SKU is named for its active ingredient: Quadruple Hyaluronic Acid 5%, Retinol Complex, BHA Liquid Exfoliant, Glycolic Acid 10%), Ulta-exclusive launch SKUs for retail-partnership theater, E.l.f.-integrated social marketing across the parent's 3M+ follower base.
- **Earned media:** Tight-loop ingredient-education content (what is azelaic acid, what is tranexamic acid, why peptides) rather than influencer-viral campaign-driven. Closer to The Ordinary's ingredient-education DNA than to Rhode's celebrity-driven moments.
- **Promo posture:** Moderate. Participates in Ulta's 21 Days of Beauty, Target Circle Week, Ulta Spring/Fall Haul events. DTC does not run sitewide discounts but uses E.l.f.'s loyalty mechanics for repurchase.

## SJS implications

1. **Naturium is the single most important shelf comparison for SJS at UBM launch.** Same retailer (Ulta), same price band ($10-$25), same skincare-plus-body category footprint, same founder-led positioning (Susan Yara = founder-voice comp to Ayesha + the SJS team). If SJS launches at UBM at $28-$45, it sits **one tier up** from Naturium's $10-$25. That is the key positioning decision: SJS needs to justify the premium over Naturium — ingredient story, packaging, ritual, formulation quality — or it risks looking like "Naturium-but-more-expensive" on the Ulta shelf.
2. **E.l.f. Beauty's portfolio playbook is the external threat to monitor.** E.l.f. acquired Naturium (Oct 2023), expanded it to Ulta (July 2024), then acquired Rhode (2025). Rhode on Sephora + Naturium on Ulta = E.l.f. owns the two most important accessible-prestige shelves. For SJS, this means the category's most sophisticated operator is now playing both sides of the prestige/mass line with brands SJS is directly benchmarking against. Any E.l.f. move — Rhode into Ulta, Naturium into Sephora, a third acquisition — immediately reshapes the SJS positioning question.
3. **Multi-category at Ulta is the velocity unlock.** Naturium's 54 SKUs at Ulta span face, body, and lip — 3 categories, broad footprint. Compare Goop (10 skincare-only SKUs). Ulta shoppers multi-category-browse in a way Sephora shoppers don't (Sephora is more skincare-category-shopper). **For SJS, adding body to the UBM launch assortment is a velocity move, not a SKU-count move.** A body wash or body serum at $18-$22 would expand the UBM shopper's basket without cannibalizing SJS's lip + skincare positioning.
4. **Review velocity at Ulta is significantly higher for lower-priced SKUs.** Naturium Plant Ceramide Cream at $24.99 pulled 462 reviews in 21 months. Goop 72-Hour Water-Cream at $68 pulled 7 reviews in 8 months. The rate difference isn't just time — it's price elasticity on the Ulta shelf. **For SJS, pricing at $25-$30 instead of $40-$50 would meaningfully accelerate UBM review accumulation** and therefore algorithmic surfacing. This is a structural tradeoff, not a branding one.
5. **Ulta-exclusive SKU architecture is a requirement, not an option.** Naturium launched Ulta with 3 exclusives (Glow Getter Body Oil, Barrier Bounce, Vitamin Bright). This gave Ulta's merchandising team product to promote. **SJS should plan a UBM-exclusive SKU from day one** — even if it's a bundle or a limited-edition variant rather than a new formulation. Ulta merchandising teams reward partners who bring them something exclusive.
6. **Sub-$15 lip as a Trojan horse for skincare consideration.** Naturium Phyto-Glow Lip Mask $12.99 + Shimmer Lip Balm $11.99 is the brand's bottom-of-funnel acquisition SKU — low price, impulse-purchase, drives basket into the skincare aisle. **SJS's $20 lip is above this range and not built to play the impulse-purchase role.** If SJS wants the impulse-lip-to-skincare conversion mechanic, SJS would need a sub-$15 lip SKU — which is a real strategic choice with COGS implications.

## Early-warning watchlist

- **E.l.f. acquiring Rhode + operating Naturium simultaneously** — watch for any cross-brand merchandising coordination at Ulta (e.g., Rhode joining Naturium at Ulta, or Naturium gaining Sephora access). Either move resets the cohort.
- **Naturium SKU count growth rate** — from 45 to 54 SKUs in 21 months (20% growth). If that accelerates past 70-80 SKUs, signals E.l.f. is doubling down on Naturium as the skincare share-take vehicle. If it plateaus, signals E.l.f. shifting skincare bet to Rhode.
- **Ulta-exclusive SKU cadence** — Naturium launched 3 Ulta exclusives at entry. If E.l.f. continues to create Ulta-exclusives at 1-2 per year pace, this hardens Naturium's Ulta positioning. Any Ulta-exclusive underperformance would signal retail-partner attention reallocation.
- **E.l.f. Skin share of Ulta vs. Naturium** — they are now both in Ulta and share the E.l.f.-Beauty merchandising calendar. Internal competition for shelf space is a real risk within E.l.f.'s own portfolio.
- **International expansion cadence** — 5 countries in year 1 of E.l.f. ownership (US + Canada + UK + Italy + Australia). Rate of further expansion signals how aggressively E.l.f. will run the brand.
- **Susan Yara bandwidth** — she is the founder voice + content engine. Any transition (co-branding into other projects, reducing involvement) would shift the earned-media calculus.
