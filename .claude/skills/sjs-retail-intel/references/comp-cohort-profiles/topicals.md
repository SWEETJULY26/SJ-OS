# Topicals

**Last refreshed:** 2026-04-20
**Refresh priority:** High
**Cohort tier:** 4 — Founder-led, Sephora-native, culturally-attuned skincare (originally tagged inclusive/founder-led; in practice operates as a Tier 2 specialty comp — recommend reclassification alongside Glow Recipe at next SKILL.md refresh)
**Why in cohort:** **Sephora's fastest-growing skincare brand** per multiple 2024-2025 sources. Founder-led by Olamide Olowe (youngest Black woman to raise $15M VC), positioned around chronic skin conditions (hyperpigmentation, eczema, acne, ingrown hairs) with strong cultural fluency. **NOT at Ulta** — Sephora + Amazon + DTC in equal thirds. The category-shaping comp for how a founder-led clean skincare brand scales in US specialty from a cold start on a budget.

---

## Snapshot

- **Founded:** August 2020 by **Olamide Olowe** (CEO, was 23 at launch) + **Claudia Teng** (co-founder, PhD-track clinical research background at Stanford). Olowe is a UCLA-educated former pre-med + track athlete; her first beauty venture was SheaGirl, co-founded while in college and exited via Unilever's Sundial Brands acquisition.
- **Positioning:** "Skincare for flare-ups" — transforming how people feel about chronic skin conditions (eczema, hyperpigmentation, acne, ingrown hairs). Clinical-grade formulas with 8+ actives per SKU, formulated specifically to work across skin tones. "Funner flare-ups" is the explicit cultural tagline. Reframes dermatology-adjacent ointments as celebrated self-care rituals.
- **Ownership:** Privately held; Olowe remains CEO + founder. **Raised $15M total VC** — the largest publicly-reported round was a $10M Series A in 2022 (the youngest-Black-woman milestone). Earlier: $2.6M pre-seed with celebrity backers Jay-Z's Marcy Venture Partners, Kelly Rowland, Gabrielle Union, Issa Rae, Yvonne Orji, Warby Parker, Casper. Olowe has since launched **Cost of Doing Business** (2025), a holding company intended to acquire and scale culturally-attuned brands.
- **Revenue:** Not publicly disclosed. Signals: "Sephora's fastest-growing skincare brand" per Beauty Independent (Sept 2025), CEW (June 2025), and Essence (Oct 2025). Launch SKUs sold out at Nordstrom in 2 hours + Sephora in 48 hours. By Year 1 at launch, raised at meaningful valuation. Brand Report "$10M brand" framing widely referenced, though this likely understates 2025 revenue given Sephora velocity.

## Product architecture

- **SKU count:** ~15-20 across Faded (hyperpigmentation), Clearly (acne), Like Butter (eczema/dry), High Roller (ingrown hair), plus eye masks, face mist, body bar, limited-edition drops
- **Hero SKUs (with live Sephora signals as of 2026-04-20):**
  - **Faded Serum for Dark Spots & Discoloration** $38 (50ml) — **2.4K reviews / 228.8K loves** — original hero, 8+ actives (tranexamic acid, alpha-arbutin, kojic acid, azelaic acid, niacinamide, glutathione, melatonin, turmeric butter, licorice root, centella asiatica). **Allure 2025 + 2024 Best of Beauty Award Winner**. Sold out at Sephora in 48 hours at launch (March 2021). **Price has increased from $36 (launch) → $38 (2026)** — 5.5% over 5 years, restrained vs. category inflation.
  - **Like Butter Moisturizer for Dry, Sensitive & Eczema-Prone Skin** $34 (50ml) — 487 reviews / 63.3K loves — **launched 2024**, richer/creamier formula than the original Like Butter Mask, with 1% colloidal oatmeal (OTC-level eczema treatment claim)
  - **Like Butter Hydrating Mask** $32 — original product, overnight hydrating mask with centella asiatica, green tea, oatmeal — still sold, now co-exists with the moisturizer variant
  - **High Roller Ingrown Hair Tonic** (rollerball) — Sephora #1 in category; Allure 2024 + 2025 Best of Beauty Award Winner; for face, armpits, bikini area; salicylic + zinc PCA + glycolic
  - **Faded Brightening & Clearing Mist** — spray version of Faded
  - **Faded Eye Masks** (Cognac colorway, 6 pairs of hydrogel) — limited-edition brightening eye masks, 15-minute treatment
  - **Faded Brightening & Cleansing Body Bar** (2-pack) — body expansion of Faded formula with kojic acid + vitamin C
  - **Clearly Breakout Kit / Acne Reset Kit** — launched October 2025 as 5th anniversary drop, co-created with licensed esthetician Tiara Willis
  - **Faded Starter Kit** — bundled entry-SKU with access to "Topicals Certified" esthetician program
- **Category mix:** Treatment-led, chronic-condition-focused. Extremely tight SKU discipline compared to Naturium (54 SKUs) or Byoma (39 Ulta SKUs) — Topicals runs 15-20 total SKUs with deep formula investment per SKU.
- **Bundle strategy:** Faded Brightening Bundle (unscented), Faded Starter Kit (with Topicals Certified program access), Acne Reset Kit, limited-edition color sets of eye masks.
- **Price ladder:** Faded $38 → Like Butter Moisturizer $34 → Like Butter Mask $32 → body bar 2-pack / High Roller / Faded Mist / Eye Masks at ~$15-$30 range. Tighter and higher-priced than Naturium/Byoma; lower than Summer Fridays/Tatcha core range.

## Marketing posture

- **Founder amplification:** Maximum. Olamide Olowe is one of the most media-visible founders in beauty — Essence cover stories, CEW features, Fortune / WWD profiles, multiple podcast episodes. Cultural authority as young Black female founder + clinical expertise (pre-med UCLA) + entrepreneurial pedigree (SheaGirl → Unilever exit).
- **Signature moves:** Astrology-based pre-launch quiz (10K emails pre-launch); Ghana brand trips for community-building; cinematic hip-hop-inspired campaigns; aughts-inspired velour tracksuits + merch (treating the brand as lifestyle, not just skincare); Topicals Certified esthetician education program (Oct 2025 launch with Tiara Willis); Cost of Doing Business holding company (2025); frequent collabs with culturally-aligned personalities (Issa Rae, Yvonne Orji as investors + vocal supporters); mental health advocacy baked into brand DNA ($250K+ donated).
- **Earned media:** Heavily Instagram + TikTok-native; community-driven rather than influencer-paid. Olowe personally is the primary brand voice. Essence + Vogue + Allure consistent editorial presence.
- **Promo posture:** Disciplined. Sephora Savings Events participation (10/15/20% by Insider tier, Auto-Replenish 5% on hero SKUs). Faded is $38 (up from $36 launch) — 5-year $2 price bump is the most disciplined pricing in the cohort. Limited-edition drops (color sets of eye masks) create scarcity/storytelling rather than discount-to-move-units. Olowe explicit strategy: DTC as test-and-learn channel, bundles with educational content attached.

## SJS implications

1. **Topicals is the cleanest founder-led-to-scale playbook study in the cohort.** Olowe went from $1,000 to Sephora's fastest-growing skincare brand in 5 years without celebrity cofounder leverage. The playbook was: (1) pre-launch community (astrology quiz, 10K emails), (2) incubator/accelerator credibility (Sephora Accelerate class), (3) tight hero-SKU focus (Faded + Like Butter — 2 SKUs carried the brand for years), (4) disciplined pricing ($36 → $38 over 5 years), (5) cultural-authority content engine. **For SJS, Olowe's founder-authenticity-at-scale playbook is more relevant than Bieber's celebrity-leverage playbook.** Ayesha's cultural fluency + the Sweet July lifestyle infrastructure maps closest to this model.
2. **Sephora-exclusive at this scale validates the Sephora moat one more time.** 7 of 10 cohort brands in US specialty are Sephora-only (Rhode, Summer Fridays, LANEIGE, YTTP, Glow Recipe, Topicals, Tatcha until 2025). Topicals specifically stayed Sephora-only through rapid scale — Olowe says "Sephora has made it easier for us to get into international markets," particularly UK. **The UBM-bypass pattern holds. SJS on UBM has continued structural whitespace from Topicals' brand-awareness halo without shelf-level competition.**
3. **Topicals runs 15-20 SKUs but earns 2.4K reviews on the hero — tight-SKU discipline validated.** Compare: Naturium 54 SKUs, Byoma 39 SKUs, but neither hero SKU exceeds Topicals' 228K Sephora loves. **The lesson: fewer SKUs with deeper formulations and stronger cultural narrative outperform broader assortments.** For SJS UBM launch, this argues against rushing to 20+ SKUs — better to launch with 6-8 strong SKUs and let them build.
4. **The $32-$38 Topicals range is exactly where SJS should be pricing the skincare hero.** Topicals Faded ($38) sits above the Naturium/Byoma cap ($19.99-$24.99) and below the Summer Fridays/Rhode hero ($45-$49). **This $30-$40 window is the accessible-prestige "specialty retail" zone.** SJS skincare hero (Castaway Cream or equivalent) should price in the $32-$42 range on UBM to match the category shape.
5. **DTC as 1/3 of revenue is a genuinely diversified channel mix.** Topicals maintains DTC share via "test and learn" limited-edition drops (color sets, bundles with education attached). **SJS has a strong DTC already; Topicals shows how to keep DTC relevant even after Sephora/Amazon scale** — make DTC the innovation/community channel, not the discount channel.
6. **The 5+2+0 condition-category split (face + body + Ulta=0) is structural.** Topicals operates in 4 condition categories (hyperpigmentation, eczema, acne, ingrown hair) across face + body + eye. No Ulta, no mass retail. This is the posture SJS is implicitly building toward — but SJS is launching at UBM, which is a different bet. **Topicals is the proof-point for Sephora-only specialty; SJS at UBM needs to prove accessible-prestige works at Ulta-scale.** These are different playbooks.
7. **Cost of Doing Business holding company (2025) is worth watching.** Olowe is building a portfolio holding co specifically to acquire and scale culturally-aligned brands. **Sweet July as a lifestyle umbrella + AC Brands as the operator is a parallel structure.** Worth tracking what Olowe's holdco acquires and how — it's a template for how SJS could extend Sweet July's brand architecture if/when additional brands launch under the umbrella.

## Early-warning watchlist

- **Ulta entry** — Topicals is the most probable Sephora-exclusive brand to break ranks if pressured to scale. Has mass distribution logic (chronic-condition category appeal to Ulta's clinical-skincare shopper). Any Ulta signal would reshape the UBM landscape for SJS materially.
- **International expansion pace** — "in other markets in the next 18 months" (Sept 2025). Watch for Australia, EU, South Africa entries. Each expansion proves the playbook + raises the acquisition attractiveness.
- **Cost of Doing Business acquisitions** — if Olowe's holdco picks up 1-2 smaller brands, signals Olowe's ambition is platform-level not single-brand. Could become a strategic template for SJS's parent-brand architecture.
- **Price ceiling breaking** — Faded moved $36 → $38 over 5 years. Any SKU that launches at $45+ would signal upmarket stretch.
- **Celebrity-founder collab** — Topicals has done cinematic campaigns with cultural figures. If a "Topicals x [named celebrity]" SKU or campaign launches, could shift the brand-energy tier.
- **M&A signal** — at current velocity + scale, Topicals is at the stage where L'Oréal, Unilever Prestige, E.l.f. Beauty would evaluate acquisition. Cost of Doing Business launch suggests Olowe prefers operator path, but watch trade press.
