# BYOMA

**Last refreshed:** 2026-04-20
**Refresh priority:** Critical
**Cohort tier:** 3 — Accessible clean down-price anchor; "masstige disrupting mass"
**Why in cohort:** Full Ulta shelf presence (39 SKUs) + Target + Sephora UK + Space NK + Boots + Selfridges. **$300M+ 2025 global retail sales** — grew from zero to $300M in ~3 years, the fastest growth trajectory in the cohort. All-under-$20 pricing with aggressive active-ingredient claims. One of the two most direct UBM/Ulta shelf competitors for SJS (alongside Naturium). Newly acquired by Bansk Group (Sept 2025) — signals sustained capital + scale ambition.

---

## Snapshot

- **Founded:** February 2022 by **Marc Elrick** (founder + CEO), under parent company **Future Beauty Labs** (also parent of Tan-Luxe, Tanologist, and Isle of Paradise self-tan brands).
- **Positioning:** "Skin-Compatible Skincare" — barrier-health focus, proprietary **Tri-Ceramide Complex™** (ceramides + cholesterol + fatty acids at 3:1:1 ratio). Elrick frames it as "luxury prestige disrupting masstige and mass" at sub-$20 price points. Vibrantly colored, recyclable monomaterial plastic packaging — engineered for Gen Z TikTok grid appeal + sustainability claim.
- **Ownership:** **Bansk Group acquired majority stake September 2025** from Yellow Wood Partners (who held the previous majority). Bansk portfolio: Amika (4th-largest prestige hair brand in US), Eva NYC, Ethique. Terms undisclosed. Marc Elrick retains minority stake + CEO role; leadership team intact; Byoma operates stand-alone within Bansk.
- **Revenue:** **Expected to close 2025 with >$300M global retail sales** (WWD Sept 2025). 2023 sales were $30-50M per Axios. Trajectory: $0 (Feb 2022) → $30-50M (2023) → >$300M (2025). ~6-10x growth 2023→2025. Elrick's stated ambition: "build the next CeraVe." Bansk expects "$300M-500M in next 3 years."

## SJS implications

1. **Byoma's $16.99 "everything is the same price" architecture is a deliberate shelf-strategy choice.** Most of the serum and moisturizer line sits at the same price point — which means the shopper doesn't compare within the brand, they just pick the one that matches their skin concern. **For SJS, the implication is the opposite: if SJS uses multi-tier pricing ($28 / $35 / $42) across its skincare line, the shopper compares within SJS before comparing to competitors.** The competitive framing changes when brand-internal price dispersion is higher. Worth testing whether SJS should flatten prices on some tiers.
2. **904 reviews on the Hydrating Serum is a realistic SJS hero-SKU target at Ulta.** Byoma's top SKU has more Ulta reviews than Tatcha's top SKU (Dewy Skin Cream, 1,213 reviews but at $25/$45/$93 multi-size and 14 months at Ulta). At $16.99 and ~3.5 years at Ulta, Byoma accumulates at roughly 260 reviews/year on the top SKU. SJS at $30+ should model 50-150 reviews/year as a realistic top-SKU velocity through the first 12 months on UBM.
3. **Ulta-exclusive SKUs are table stakes, validated again.** Byoma has 2+ Ulta exclusives (Foaming Rice Deep Cleanser, 3-piece Trio bundle). Naturium has 3+. Both brands earned full-shelf placement partly by bringing Ulta differentiated product. SJS on UBM should have at least 1 UBM-exclusive SKU from launch.
4. **Bansk Group's acquisition strengthens the Byoma competitive moat for 2026-2027.** Pre-acquisition Byoma had 3-year velocity proof. Post-acquisition, Byoma has R&D + infrastructure + Amika-playbook scale coming. **For SJS, this means Byoma is not a 3-year startup with hype — it's a funded scaling competitor that will continue to roll out new SKUs, international expansion, and promotional aggression through 2027.** Monitor Byoma's SKU-cadence and international-entry pace.
5. **Sub-$20 ceiling puts real pressure on SJS's mid-tier pricing.** Byoma's entire skincare assortment ceilings at $19.99 (SPF). That's roughly half of where SJS is currently positioned. For an Ulta shopper looking at "barrier-boosting serum with ceramides," Byoma at $16.99 vs. a SJS-equivalent at $30-$40 is a 2x price gap. SJS has to be clearly better-positioned — on ritual, ingredients, packaging, or brand story — to justify the difference. The brand-experience + Ayesha-story differentiator must do real work, or the SKU just looks overpriced.
6. **"The next CeraVe" is an explicit ambition worth taking seriously.** Elrick's CeraVe-aspiration is not marketing puffery. CeraVe went from ~$100M at L'Oréal acquisition (2017) to ~$2B+ today. If Bansk + Byoma execute anywhere close to that trajectory, Byoma becomes a category-defining brand by 2028. **SJS should not benchmark against Byoma's current state — should benchmark against Byoma's 2027-2028 projected state.** That's a very different shelf competitor.

## Early-warning watchlist

- **Bansk investment cadence under new ownership** — any new SKU launches, category entries (body expansion, makeup/color), or geographic expansion within 6 months of acquisition signals aggressive Bansk operator posture. Watch WWD/BoF for announcements.
- **International scaling** — Byoma UK is at Sephora, Boots, Space NK, Selfridges simultaneously. Watch for US Sephora entry (currently notable absence) and further EU expansion. Any US Sephora entry reshapes the category.
- **Ulta SKU count growth rate** — from 6 (Oct 2022) to 39 (April 2026) = 6.5x in 3.5 years. If the rate accelerates, E.l.f. / Naturium face meaningful pressure.
- **Price ceiling breaking** — Byoma has held sub-$20 aggressively. If the brand introduces a sub-brand or a premium line at $25-$35, signals upmarket stretch that directly encroaches SJS's zone.
- **CeraVe-vs-Byoma share data** — Bansk will likely publish Circana / Nielsen share data as ammunition. Watch for any "Byoma is taking share from CeraVe" narrative in trade press.
- **Legal / regulatory on "Tri-Ceramide Complex" claim** — proprietary-ingredient-name marketing is FTC-sensitive. Any challenge to the clinical claims would be instructive for the broader barrier-care category.
