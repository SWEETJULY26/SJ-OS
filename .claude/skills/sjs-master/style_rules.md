# Style Rules

**Use:** All written output across all skills. Read at runtime when drafting any communication, status update, document, or status comment.

**Source priority:** Organization instructions take precedence. Sweet July Skin brand voice (per the brand skill) overlays on SJS-facing output. Internal preferences below fill the rest.

---

## Banned words (from org instructions)

These never appear in output, regardless of context:

```
delve, leverage, utilize, facilitate, optimize, implement, streamline,
empower, harness, unlock, unleash, foster, elevate, showcase, endeavor,
embark, spearhead, bolster, revolutionize, navigate (metaphor), robust,
seamless, comprehensive, vital, crucial, pivotal, multifaceted, nuanced,
intricate, ever-evolving, ever-changing, cutting-edge, state-of-the-art,
innovative, dynamic, holistic, profound, meticulous, daunting, tapestry,
realm, landscape (metaphor), ecosystem (metaphor), paradigm, synergy,
methodology, journey, voyage
```

Plain equivalents: use, help, improve, set up, change, start, lead, important, key, strong, full, careful, new, tough, area, approach.

---

## Banned openers, transitions, closings

**Openers never used:**
- "In today's fast-paced world,"
- "In today's digital age,"
- "In today's ever-evolving landscape,"
- "In the world of X,"
- "When it comes to,"
- "At the end of the day."

**Transitions never used:** furthermore, moreover, additionally, indeed, notably, it's worth noting, it's important to note, importantly, interestingly, subsequently, consequently, accordingly.

**Closings never used:** "In conclusion," "In summary," "Ultimately," "At its core," "The bottom line is." Stop when the point is made.

---

## Banned patterns

- "It's not X — it's Y" reframes. State the point directly.
- Self-posed rhetorical questions answered immediately.
- Three back-to-back tricolons or parallel three-part lists.
- Starting 3+ sentences with the same subject or pronoun.

---

## Banned clichés

```
unlock the potential of, unleash the power of, harness the power of,
push the boundaries of, at the forefront of, a gateway to, bridging
the gap, navigate the complexities, lay the groundwork for, foster
a culture of, a testament to, speaks volumes, deep dive, let's dive
in, let's delve into, let's explore.
```

---

## Formatting

- Default to paragraphs, not bullets. Bullets only for genuine lists (parallel, 3+ items).
- No bolded lead words on bullets ("Clarity:", "Impact:").
- No headers on short docs under ~500 words unless navigation is needed.
- Don't announce structure ("Here's what I found:"). Just deliver it.
- Bold sparingly — 1-2 key phrases per section max.
- No vibe emojis (🚀 🌟 🎯 ✨) in professional writing.

## AC Brands–specific rules

### Section titles
**Never use parentheses in section titles.** Inline parentheses for asides are fine; section headers go without.

### Vendor terminology
Never use **"vessel."** Use the specific component: tube, bottle, jar, pump, cap, carton (primary or secondary), sachet, stick. Ask if unsure.

For the lip program, use the precise turnkey vocabulary documented in `tool_patterns.md` → "Lip program vendor logic." Turnkey tinted, non-turnkey, and "all lip tubes are HCT" — these terms have specific meanings and shouldn't drift.

### Status update titles
Asana project status updates always use this title format:

```
[RAG label] — [Month Day] Update
```

Examples:
- `At Risk — April 14 Update`
- `Off Track — April 14 Update`
- `On Track — May 1 Update`

Where `RAG label` is one of: `On Track`, `At Risk`, `Off Track`, `On Hold`, `Achieved`. Match the field options in the Asana UI exactly.

### Design notes
Design notes in any output are **design-only** — visual specs, layout, color, type. No operational context mixed in. Operational context belongs in body content, not in the design treatment of it.

## Tone

No flattery ("great question"), no over-hedging ("it's worth considering"), no false profundity ("at its core"). Write how a smart, specific person talks to a colleague. Vary sentence lengths. Be concrete.

For external-facing copy (press, Ulta, Sephora, investors), lean on commas and periods — heavy em dash use is the strongest visible AI tell.

For Sweet July Skin brand-facing output, the warmer "Irie" tone applies on top of these rules. The brand skill has the specifics.

---

## Concision

Initial drafts that include too much detail get cut back. High-level, concise communication is preferred. Default to less, expand on request.
